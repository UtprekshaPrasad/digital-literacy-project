# Case Study: Ransomware Attack
Ransomware is a type of cybercrime where attackers block access to a computer system or encrypt files and demand payment (ransom) to restore access. Ransomware attacks are major threat, that paralyzes organizations by encrypting data and demanding payment, often disrupting critical infrastructure ans healthcare. A common example involves phishing emails or malicious downloads.

In a typical scenario, an employee of a small company receives an email that appears to be from a trusted vendor. The email contains an attachment labeled “Invoice.” When the employee opens it, a hidden malware file installs ransomware on the system. The malware quickly spreads through the company’s network, encrypting all important files. A message then appears on the screen demanding payment, usually in cryptocurrency, within a limited time.

Small businesses, hospitals, and individuals with limited cybersecurity awareness are usually targeted because they often lack strong security systems. In many cases, organizations that rely heavily on data for daily operations are especially vulnerable.

The consequences can be severe. The company may lose access to critical data, leading to business disruption, financial loss, and damage to reputation. Even if the ransom is paid, there is no guarantee that the attackers will restore access. Additionally, sensitive data may be leaked or misused.

This case highlights the importance of cybersecurity measures such as regular data backups, employee awareness training, antivirus software, and avoiding suspicious links or attachments.
